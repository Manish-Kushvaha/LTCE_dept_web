GDSC (Google Developer Student Clubs)

Google Developer Clubs, formerly known as Google Developer Groups (GDGs), are community-driven groups for developers interested in Google technologies. These clubs typically organize events like tech talks, workshops, hackathons, and study jams focused on various Google products and platforms such as Android, Firebase, Google Cloud Platform, TensorFlow, and more. Members of these clubs often collaborate, share knowledge, and learn from each other, fostering a supportive environment for growth and innovation in the developer community. You can find more information about Google Developer Clubs on the official Google Developers website or by searching for local chapters in your area.


What is Google Developer Student Clubs ?

Explore more than 2,100 college and university chapters across more than 100 countries. Meet and learn with other budding developers and build solutions for local businesses and communities with Google technology.

Why to Connect :

Meet students interested in developer technologies at your college or university. All are welcome, including those with diverse backgrounds and different majors.

What you Learn :

Learn about a range of technical topics and gain new skills through hands-on workshops, events, talks, and project-building activities online and in-person.

Grow:

Apply new learnings to build great solutions for local problems. Advance your skills, career, and network. Give back to your community by helping others learn.

“Showing more representation in tech with GDSC greatly increases our confidence, innovation, and performance. Especially in academic environments, having a sense of belonging and community for students can lead to success in tech together.”

CESA Club

CESA stands for Computer Engineering Students Association. It's an organization typically found in universities or colleges that brings together students studying computer engineering. CESA often organizes events, workshops, and activities to enhance students' learning, networking, and professional development within the field of computer engineering.

Computer Engineering Students Association (CESA) represents the Computer Engineering students within the Computer Engineering Department of Lokmanya Tilak College of Engineering. The primary goal of CESA-CSI is to provide a platform for our students to enhance their academic ,professional and social experiences .The Computer Engineering Students Association Council at Lokmanya Tilak College of Engineering orchestrated a triumphant open mic celebration for India's 75th Independence Day, showcasing unity and respect. Women's Day was joyously celebrated with fun activities, including musical chairs and games, fostering camaraderie among female faculties. The CESA-CSI Council, vital for technical events, elected its 2022-23 members through offline interviews, emphasizing skills and commitment. A felicitation event on September 22 acknowledged academic achievers and launched the Geeky Devolution.exe coding club. The CESA-CSI Council also organized a successful online quiz competition on Instagram, recognizing Independence Day with traditional outfits. Furthermore, the singing and drawing competitions paid homage to Lata Mangeshkar and showcased students' creativity, aligning with CESA's commitment to promoting arts and culture. The events collectively highlighted the college's vibrant and inclusive celebratory spirit.

Geeky Devolution Coding Club

22nd September, 2022

The CSI council of Lokmanya Tilak College of Engineering (LTCE) recently launched Geeky

Devolution, the first ever coding club of the college. The club was founded with the aim of helping students hone their programming skills and build up their knowledge base. The program was presented by the Computer Science and Information (CSI) Council, and was launched with the support and cooperation of the college faculty and administration. To join the club, students must demonstrate their programming and coding proficiency, as well as their knowledge of computer science, general IT, and related fields. The primary focus of Geeky Devolution is to provide a platform for students to learn and explore the world of coding and the club encourages students to number of seminars, workshops, and hackathons to help students gain experience and hone their programming skills. Some of the topics covered in the seminars include web development, app development, data science, and machine learning. The hackathons, meanwhile, allowed participants to work on their own projects and showcase their coding skills. Overall, Geeky Devolution's launch by the CSI Council of LTCE was a huge success. It provided students with an invaluable opportunity to learn programming, network with industry professionals, and find job opportunities.
